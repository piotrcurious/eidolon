Microtonal — the "diatonic scale" concept now generalizes to any EDO via MOS (moment-of-symmetry) construction: stack the best available approximation of a 3:2 fifth seven times, sort, and you get a proper 2-step-size scale in any tuning (verified clean for 12/17/19/22/24/31/41/53-EDO). Chord qualities are now defined as target just-intonation ratios in cents (5:4 for the major third, 7:4 for the dominant seventh, 11:8 for a real #11, etc.) mapped to the nearest step of whichever EDO is active — at 12-EDO this reproduces the exact original chord tables bit-for-bit. Concretely: 12-EDO's #11 misses the true 11:8 ratio by 49 cents; 24-EDO gets within 1.3 cents. Tuning select has 8 curated EDOs plus a free-entry custom option (5–72).
Jazz — Style: Jazz swaps the triads for proper 7th-chord qualities and adds 5 secondary-dominant states (V7/ii, V7/iii, V7/IV, V7/V, V7/vi, and the minor-key equivalents) that resolve to their targets with strong (~85%+) but not absolute probability — still a genuine first-order Markov chain, no lookahead grammar. Tritone substitution is a post-process that recolors a dominant a half-octave away while preserving its resolution target (theoretically exact, since that's literally what tritone subs do). Extensions (9/#9/b9, #11, 13/b13) are added per-chord with mutual exclusion within each tension family so you never get clashing b9+9 simultaneously.
Editing — tap any chord to reassign its function or lock it against future regeneration. Progression and voice/spectral generation now run on separately-seeded RNG streams specifically so editing a chord never perturbs the phrase or spectral wandering elsewhere; edits re-render audio immediately.
All three cross-checked for exact backward compatibility against the original system, then stress-tested across every EDO × jazz-on/off × mode combination with no NaNs or clipping.
